Pros & Cons of EVs

Overview

PROS

  • Better parking spots
  • Maintenance
  • Performance Benefits
  • NO MORE getting gas

CONS

  • Driving Range
  • Battery Charge time
  • Limited Charging Infrastructure
  • Battery Cost

Pros - What are the advantages of driving a fully-electric vehicle?

More and more places have dedicated Parking for electric vehicles now. The spots are wider too so less likely to get door dings!

No more oil changes or worrying about transmission or radiator fluid. The only Maintenance you really have to do is changing the cabin air filter and windshield washer fluid. Regenerative braking also extends the life of your brake pads.

What is Regenerative Braking? Click for article

Electric vehicles have Performance Benefits over gas vehicles. An electric motor is able to give the EV instantaneous torque, which leads to strong and smooth acceleration. It feels like you're in a roller coaster!

Everytime we come home we plug in the charger which means NO MORE getting gas! So the only time I go to QT now is when I want some tea!

Cons - What are the disadvantages of driving an electric vehicle?

Driving Range goes down faster on EVs especially if you have a heavy foot or just using your A/C.

You can charge your car with a regular wall outlet like the one you use to charge your phone but Battery charge time will literally take 1 to 2 days if you are below 20%. Investing in installing a level 2 charger will help dramatically.

Tesla has continued to increase charging stations but still Limited Charging Infrastructure compared to traditional gas stations. If you plan on taking your EV on a roadtrip prepare to plan the trip around charging stations.

Under warranty batteries are covered till 100,00 miles or below after that expect to pay around $10,000 - $20,000 for a Battery replacement cost.
